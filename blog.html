üí≠ Existential Ramble: On Being a Professional Human

January 1, 2025 Deep Thoughts
ü§î Philosophical Mood: Activated

Happy New Year, internet! Started 2025 with a cup of Earl Grey and some thoughts that felt too big for Twitter but too small for a dissertation, so here we are.

The Professional Human Paradox

I've been thinking a lot lately about this whole concept of being a "professional." Like, what does that even mean? That I've successfully trained myself to sit still for 8 hours a day and respond to emails with appropriate levels of enthusiasm about things I don't actually care about?

Don't get me wrong - I'm not anti-work. I respect people who find genuine fulfillment in their careers. But somewhere along the way, we collectively decided that your job is supposed to be your identity, your passion, AND your primary source of meaning. That's... a lot of pressure for what is essentially an economic exchange.

Revolutionary idea: What if work was just work, and we found meaning in other places? What if "work-life balance" actually meant that work was just one small part of a much richer life?

The Frog Philosophy

This is where my frog obsession actually makes sense (stick with me here). Frogs don't have careers. They don't have LinkedIn profiles or five-year plans. They just exist, adapt to their environment, and seem pretty content doing frog things.

Obviously, I'm not suggesting we all become literal frogs (though the idea has its appeal). But maybe there's something to be learned from creatures that seem to have figured out how to just... be.

The Experiment Continues

This crowdfunding experiment isn't really about the money (though the money would be nice). It's about testing whether there's another way to exist in our current economic system. Can you fund a lifestyle of intentional living through the kindness of internet strangers? Can you opt out of traditional career climbing and still be okay?

So far, the answer seems to be "maybe?" which is honestly more promising than I expected when I started this whole thing.

Anyway, that's enough existential rambling for one morning. Time to make another cup of tea and tend to my plants like the professional human I definitely am. üê∏‚òï

üéÆ Cozy Game Review: A Short Hike

December 28, 2024 Game Review
üèîÔ∏è Cozy Factor: Maximum

Sometimes you need a game that feels like a warm blanket for your soul. "A Short Hike" is exactly that game.

üèîÔ∏è

What It Is

You play as a little bird who's trying to get cell phone reception at the top of a mountain. That's it. That's the whole premise, and it's perfect.

Why It's Perfect

There's no combat, no time pressure, no achievements to unlock. You just wander around a beautiful pixelated mountain, help other cute animals with small tasks, and gradually make your way to the summit. It's the video game equivalent of a gentle nature walk.

The music is gorgeous, the pixel art is charming, and the whole experience takes about 2 hours. It's like the developers said "what if we made a game that's just... nice?" and then actually did it.

Perfect for: Sunday afternoons, post-work decompression, or any time you need to remember that not everything has to be stressful or competitive.

This is exactly the kind of game I want to fund more of with this whole experiment. Games that prioritize peace over adrenaline, wonder over winning. The world needs more "A Short Hike" and less "Call of Duty," in my humble frog opinion.
