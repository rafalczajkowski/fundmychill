The Real Story

I graduated university with a degree I thought I wanted, worked a few jobs, got a postgrad degree, and earned a few certifications. But I never really felt truly interested in these roles. It's like wearing shoes a size too small - technically doable, but deeply uncomfortable.

I'm not lazy (well, maybe a little), but I am someone who values intentional living over constant modern productivity. I'd rather spend my days perfecting tea brewing techniques, reading books that have nothing to do with my "career development," listening to music, and maybe learning to paint watercolor frogs. I know we all need to contribute to society, but I'm just really not passionate about typical careers and I don't have any huge goals or ambitions. I just want a simple, cozy life. So you won't catch me wasting money on expensive jewelry or a fancy car. Although I would like to have a Volkswagen T1...

What I Actually Do

Right now, I work in social media while dreaming of financial independence. In my free time, I enjoy staring at the garden, experimenting with different tea blends (although my favorites are English Breakfast and Earl Grey), playing retro games, and trying to learn languages. I also enjoy traveling.

I also built this website, which was honestly more fun than anything I've done at my day job in months.

ü§î Frequently Asked Questions

Are you actually serious about this?
100% serious, though I maintain a sense of humor about it. I genuinely believe there are better ways to live than the traditional 9-5 grind for people like me, and I'm willing to try unconventional methods to achieve that. I understand that many people love their careers, have families to support, or find fulfillment in traditional work - but that's just not me.

Do you actually like frogs?
I do! They're peaceful, content, and mind their own business. Plus, they're perfectly happy sitting still for long periods of time, which is basically my career goal.
What's your backup plan?
Keep working my day job while building up savings and looking for remote work opportunities. This experiment is a bonus, not my only strategy for escaping the traditional workplace.
